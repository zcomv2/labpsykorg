Definition of Cypherpunk

Cypherpunks are a Digital Life Race, an evolution of the cyberpunk movement that has refined its focus on security, privacy, and continuous software improvement. They differentiate themselves from traditional cyberpunks through their discipline and precision: "They sharpen the katana more", meaning they do not just create, but optimize, patch, and reinforce code to make it more efficient, secure, and accessible. Cypherpunks are specialists in polishing code, enhancing application performance, and ensuring the integrity of digital information. Their work revolves around securing interfaces as safe communication channels, preventing censorship, surveillance, and vulnerabilities that could compromise privacy. Committed to the freedom of knowledge, Cypherpunks believe in teaching and sharing wisdom. They do not seek profit but rather collective progress, distributing their developments under GPL and public-use licenses. They understand that security and privacy are not achieved overnight, so they continuously practice, experiment, and refine their craft. Cypherpunks do not care whether the software they create is accepted or understood by everyone. They know that code is indestructible and free. In an increasingly centralized digital world, their existence is crucial: their code and distributed networks are the last hope for truly free and high-quality information.
